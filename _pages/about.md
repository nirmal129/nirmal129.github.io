Hi, I'm Nirmal N. Patel. I am a PhD student in Computational Science, Engineering, and Mathematics (CSEM) at the Oden Institute at The University of Texas at Austin, where I also completed my B.S. in Physics Honors. My research has evolved from PhysicsML to language model pre-training, and more recently to robust alignment and post-training, with the broader goal of building reliable, data-efficient AI systems capable of long-horizon reasoning.

I like approaching ML from both empirical and theoretical angles: understanding scaling behavior, failure modes, and algorithmic structure, then turning those insights into practical training recipes. Recently, I have been especially interested in robust alignment, agent post-training, and richer feedback mechanisms for long-horizon reasoning. I have been fortunate to collaborate with Sai Surya Duvvuri, Fei Wang, Nilesh Gupta, Inderjit S. Dhillon, Pablo Laguna, and Aycin Aykutalp.
